### §01 DEFINITION

Multinational FMCG companies with established Vietnam operations, regional or global commerce/digital functions, and active interest in scaling social commerce as a growth lever in SEA. This segment is defined by the tension between regional consistency requirements (driven by global/regional HQ) and local Vietnam execution — a tension VinaCapital sits at the center of.

These companies are post-pilot: they've tested TikTok Shop or Shopee Live, seen early signals, and are now evaluating whether to scale. The question is not "should we do social commerce" but "how do we scale it without losing brand consistency and without adding more vendor complexity."

### §02 BUYING SIGNALS

- Signs a contract with a new KOL agency or requests RFP for "integrated social commerce" — signals they're consolidating
- Hires a Regional Commerce Director or creates a new "Head of Social Commerce" role at country or SEA level
- Posts job ads for "Livestream Operations Manager" or "KOC Program Lead" — DIY is getting too slow
- Announces a GMV-linked growth target in annual report or investor call
- L'Oréal-pattern: runs a Tet or 11.11 hero campaign, sees spike, then wants to make it repeatable
- Attends TikTok Shop / Shopee partner events and engages with platform account managers at VP level
- Publicly quotes social commerce as a strategic priority in regional marketing leadership interview

### §03 PAIN POINTS

- **Vendor fragmentation**: 4-6 vendors covering KOL, content, e-commerce, measurement — zero unified view
- **Attribution chaos**: KOL content drives sales but the numbers don't add up cleanly across platforms
- **Operational scale gap**: Can run one great livestream; cannot run 50 consistently
- **Regional/local tension**: HQ wants consistency, VN team needs flexibility — no vendor spans both
- **Talent gap**: Can't find VN talent who knows both creator culture and commerce operations
- **Measurement credibility**: Finance teams demanding incrementality proof that vendors can't produce

### §04 DECISION PROCESS

Initiated by Regional Commerce or Digital Director (champion). Evaluated by Brand Manager (brand equity lens) and Country GM (local execution fit). Approved by Regional CMO or GM (budget) and Procurement (commercial terms).

Timeline: 8-14 weeks from first meeting to signed contract. Pilots often precede full contracts. Procurement adds 2-4 weeks at the end.

Budget cycle: Annual planning Aug-Oct, mid-year reforecast Jan-Feb. Best entry windows: September (planning season) and early Q1 (reforecast).

### §05 PRIMARY DECISION-MAKER

Regional Commerce / Digital Director. Title variants: Regional Commerce Director, APAC Head of Commerce, SEA Commerce Director. Owns regional GMV P&L. Bonus tied 40-60% to GMV target. Needs peer references from other MNCs. Evaluates vendors on: execution credibility, measurement rigor, vendor consolidation potential. Key fear: picking a vendor that looks good in the pitch but executes thin in production.

### §06 VALUE PROPOSITIONS

- One contract replaces 3 vendor relationships — direct reduction in coordination overhead
- Full-stack ownership: creator sourcing → briefing → execution → measurement, from one P&L
- Proven MNC-fit: L'Oréal CPD VN case study provides peer-comparable proof at scale
- Operational depth at scale: studios, trained hosts, repeatable ops — not just access to creators
- Attribution-first measurement framework that maps to finance's incrementality requirements
- Regional expandability: VN proof → SEA replication pathway with same operational model

### §07 QUALIFICATION CRITERIA

**Must-haves**
- VN legal entity with local team (not pure export model)
- Annual VN revenue >$50M or regional SEA revenue >$200M
- Active or imminently launching TikTok Shop or Shopee presence
- Decision-maker structure includes regional commerce/digital director role

**Strong fits**
- L'Oréal, Unilever, Reckitt, P&G, Henkel, Beiersdorf, Colgate, Haleon
- Currently running fragmented KOL + media + e-commerce vendors separately
- Regional GMV target explicitly stated internally or publicly
- Open RFP or "vendor consolidation" initiative underway

**Disqualifiers**
- Pure D2C without retail distribution (different economics)
- No VN entity — export-only model
- Sub-scale brands (<$10M VN revenue)
- Company policy prohibits working with boutique vendors (only WPP-tier agency approved)

### §08 COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

**KOL agencies** (e.g., iClick, Emerald Asia): Only cover creator sourcing and content. No commerce conversion. We out-scope them on the conversion layer and measurement rigor.

**Media agencies** (GroupM units, Publicis): Cover paid media amplification. Don't own the creator network or live commerce operations. We complement (often work alongside) rather than replace.

**E-commerce enablers** (Lazada partners, Shopee ecosystem players): Manage storefronts and logistics. Don't bring the creator audience or live commerce capability. We are upstream.

**In-house team build-out**: The "do it ourselves" alternative. VinaCapital responds with time-to-scale and talent availability argument — building takes 12-18 months, market moves in 3.

**Other full-stack players**: Limited credible regional competition at MNC scale in VN. Most full-stack claims are overstated — studio visits expose the gap.

### §09 ENGAGEMENT APPROACH

**Preferred channels**: Warm intro via LinkedIn or mutual contact. LinkedIn DM (personalized, short) if no warm intro. Email for substantive content after first connection. WhatsApp for follow-up once relationship is established.

**Tone**: Professional, direct, executive-summary-first. Lead with outcome, not product. No salesy enthusiasm — lands as amateur.

**Recommended first touch**: Share a specific insight relevant to their brand category + request a short call to explore fit. Avoid generic "we'd love to connect" openers.

**What NOT to do**: Lead with creator network size. Use "synergy" or "leverage" language. Imply their current decisions were wrong. Pitch in an unexpected setting before credibility is established.

### §10 SAMPLE COMPANIES

- **L'Oréal VN** — Active client. Full validation of model. Consumer Products Division is flagship reference.
- **Unilever VN** — Strong fit, Beauty & Wellbeing division evaluating. Procurement is the gating factor.
- **Reckitt VN** — Fit, but Health division has different buying rhythm than FMCG. Slow cycle.
- **P&G VN** — Tier 1 target. Currently using media agency for creator work — consolidation angle.
- **Beiersdorf VN (Nivea)** — Strong regional D2C shift underway. Commerce Director hire in 2024 signals intent.
- **Henkel VN** — Mid-tier fit. Smaller VN presence but active on TikTok Shop.
- **Haleon VN** — Health/OTC focus. Adjacent to core FMCG but commerce motion is different.

### §11 HYPOTHESES

- We believe that MNC accounts who have already run one successful social commerce campaign are 3x more likely to consolidate vendors within 6 months — because the ops chaos becomes visible after the first hero campaign. We'll know we're right when our sales data shows shorter time-to-close for accounts with prior campaign history vs. greenfield.

- We believe that the Regional Commerce Director's relationship with Procurement is the primary deal-accelerator or deal-killer, more than the ICP fit itself. We'll know we're right when deal velocity analysis shows Procurement engagement date as the best predictor of close date.

- We believe that a studio visit converts more director-level stakeholders than any deck, because it closes the credibility gap that every competitor fails on. We'll know we're right when we track conversion rates at proposal stage for deals where a studio visit did vs. didn't happen.

### §12 WATCH LIST

- **TikTok Shop algorithm changes**: Any shift in VN live commerce algorithm significantly changes creator ROI math. Monitoring quarterly.
- **Regional procurement consolidation**: MNCs globalizing their agency rosters (Omnicom, WPP holding company agreements) could lock out boutique vendors at contract level — not content level.
- **Platform diversification**: Brands moving livestream budgets from TikTok to Instagram Live or YouTube Shopping if TikTok Shop VN engagement metrics soften.
- **Local champion rise**: Vietnamese brands (Cocoon, Sunhouse) getting more sophisticated in social commerce, making MNCs feel competitive pressure from below.
